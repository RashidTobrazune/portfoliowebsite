Sprocket Central Pty Ltd, a medium size bikes & cycling accessories organisation, approached me in KPMG’s Lighthouse & Innovation Team. Sprocket Central Pty Ltd is keen to learn more about KPMG’s expertise in its Analytics, Information & Modelling team. Particularly, I speak about how the team can effectively analyse the datasets to help Sprocket Central Pty Ltd grow its business. Primarily, Sprocket Central Pty Ltd needs help with its customer and transactions data. The organisation has a large dataset relating to its customers, but their team is unsure how to effectively analyse it to help optimise its marketing strategy. However, in order to support the analysis, you speak to the Associate Director for some ideas and she advised that “the importance of optimising the quality of customer datasets cannot be underestimated. The better the quality of the dataset, the better chance you will be able to use it drive company growth.” The client provided KPMG with 3 datasets: Customer Demographic Customer Addresses Transactions data in the past 3 months I decided to start the preliminary data exploration and identify ways to improve the quality of Sprocket Central Pty Ltd’s data.

Goal

There were 3 goals in all. The first was to draft an email to the client identifying data quality issues and strategies to mitigate them.
The second was to develop a presentation that outlines the approach in targeting new customers based on demographics and other attributes.
The last was to Create a dashboard for the client that specifies who they should be targeting in their customer list as well as the broader market segment.

Results

The report provides actionable recommendations and next steps, supported by solid reasoning. It enables informed decision-making, and process improvement.

What I learnt

3. How visualisations such as interactive dashboards help highlight key findings and convey ideas in a succinct manner.

Case Procedure


1. Data Cleaning

Data quality issues and mitigation
Throughout the data analysis process, I encountered certain data quality issues and implemented strategies to address them effectively. Additionally, I provided recommendations to prevent these issues from recurring, thereby enhancing the accuracy of the data used for making informed business decisions. 1. Customer IDs were found in the 'Transactions' and 'Customer Address' tables that were not present in the Customer Demographic' table. Mitigation: To maintain consistency, ensure that all tables correspond to the same period, and only customers listed in the 'Customer Master' will be used for model training.
2. Some records contain empty values in columns like brand of purchase or job title. Mitigation: Records with a small number of empty fields are filtered out from the training set; otherwise, imputation is performed based on the training dataset distribution.
3. Inconsistent values were observed for the same attribute, such as "QUEENSLAND" represented as "QLD”. Mitigation: Regular expressions were employed to standardize these values for consistency. Recommendation: Implement drop-down lists for data entry to prevent multiple representations of the same value.
4. Gender records labeled as 'U' were replaced with “UNIDENTIFIED”.
5. Date format were also not same for most. Mitigation: the date format should always be one format.
6. Inconsistent data types were identified for the same attribute, with some fields as numeric values and others as strings. Mitigation: Selected records were converted from characters to numeric, and non-numeric characters were removed from strings.
Recommendation: Ensure that fact tables in the database enforce data type constraints to maintain consistency. Moving forward, our team will continue with data cleaning, standardization, and transformation for model analysis.

Interactive Dashboard

The visualization dashboard crafted for the client serves as a comprehensive guide for targeted customer engagement and market segmentation. With 3,999 customers in focus, the dashboard encapsulates key insights: Total Revenue: Displaying a total revenue of $2,000,000 provides a clear understanding of the financial scope.
Brand Distribution: A breakdown of the seven brands allows the client to discern which brands contribute most significantly to revenue and customer engagement.
Gender Demographics: Highlighting the gender distribution among customers reveals that there are more female customers than males, offering valuable insights for tailored marketing strategies.
Regional Distribution: New South Wales emerges as the region with the highest customer concentration, enabling the client to refine targeting strategies for this particular market.
This visually intuitive dashboard empowers the client to make informed decisions, identify lucrative market segments, and optimize their approach for enhanced customer engagement and revenue generation.


In analyzing the dataset, a focus on the top 10 high-value customers unveils critical insights for strategic decision-making. These customers, characterized by their significant contribution to revenue, represent a lucrative segment deserving special attention.
Exploring the demographics of these top customers, such as gender, age, or location, enables the client to tailor marketing campaigns more precisely. By focusing on the top 10 high-value customers, the client can strategically allocate resources, and enhance customer satisfaction, ultimately driving sustained revenue growth.
